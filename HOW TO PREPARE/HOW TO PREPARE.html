Scenario:

After turning on your television you found out that a Signal 3 Typhoon will hit your location later 7pm. You hear that it's advised for you to go to the nearest evacuation site since the typhoons is very strong. Here is the thigns you have to do:

Before The Typhoon:

1. Stay informed: Keep yourself updated about the weather conditions and warnings issued by the local authorities. Follow reliable sources of information like the national weather service or emergency management agencies.

2. Stock up on supplies: Have enough food, water, and essential supplies like batteries, flashlights, and first-aid kits to last at least three days. Make sure to secure your home and move outdoor objects indoors or tie them down.

3. Identify evacuation routes: Know the nearest evacuation centers and routes in case of an emergency. Familiarize yourself with the location and plan ahead for transportation.

4. Make a communication plan: Establish a communication plan with your family and friends. Have a pre-designated meeting place, and keep important documents like identification, passports, and insurance policies in a waterproof container.

During The Typhoon:

When on the Evacuation Site:

1. Stay informed: Stay up to date with the latest weather updates and news on the typhoon. Listen to the announcements made by the authorities and follow their instructions.

2. Stay safe: Remain inside the designated evacuation area and avoid going outside unless it is absolutely necessary. Keep away from windows and doors, and take shelter in a safe area in case of strong winds or flying debris.

3. Keep emergency supplies: Make sure you have enough food, water, and other essential supplies to last you for the duration of the typhoon. Keep a first-aid kit, flashlights, and battery-powered radios or devices for communication.

4. Cooperate with others: Work together with others in the evacuation site and help each other out as needed. Follow any designated evacuation routes and keep your area clean and tidy.

5. Stay calm: Typhoons can be scary, but it is important to remain calm and not panic. This will help you make rational decisions and stay safe.

When not on the Evacuation Site:

1. Stay indoors: Stay inside a sturdy building, away from windows and doors. Avoid going outside during the storm, as strong winds and debris can cause serious harm.

2. Turn off utilities: Turn off all gas, electricity, and water supplies to avoid fire and flooding.

3. Monitor news and weather updates: Stay tuned to the latest news and weather updates to know when it's safe to leave the shelter.

4. Be prepared for power outages: Have a backup power source like a generator or battery-powered devices and avoid using candles, as they can start a fire.

After The Typhoon:

1. Assess the damage: Check your home and surroundings for damage, and report any structural damage to the local authorities.

2. Beware of hazards: Be aware of potential hazards like fallen power lines, debris, and floodwater. Stay away from damaged buildings and structures.

3. Seek medical attention: If you or anyone in your family is injured, seek medical attention immediately.

4. Clean up: Begin cleaning up your home and surroundings, and dispose of debris properly. Avoid using contaminated water and wear protective gear like gloves and masks.

Things you have to bring when going to the Evacuation Site:

1. Emergency supplies: Bring enough food, water, and medication for at least three days, as well as a first-aid kit, blankets, and pillows.

2. ersonal hygiene items: Bring toiletries, such as toothbrush, toothpaste, soap, and towels, as well as hand sanitizer, wet wipes, and tissue paper.

3. Clothing: Bring enough clothing to last for a few days, including comfortable and warm clothes, raincoats, and shoes. Make sure you have extra pairs of socks and underwear.

4. Communication and entertainment devices: Bring a charged mobile phone and charger, as well as a battery-powered radio, a book, or other forms of entertainment.

5. Important documents: Bring important documents, such as identification cards, passports, and insurance papers, in a waterproof container.

6. Cash and valuables: Bring enough cash for emergency situations, as well as important valuables like jewelry, laptops, or cameras.
